Overview

A collection of 3D spatial captures showcasing different photogrammetry and Gaussian splatting techniques. From CAA-approved drone flights around London landmarks to studio-based ultra-high-poly object scanning, these captures demonstrate professional workflows for heritage documentation, product visualisation, and environmental scanning.

Tower Bridge (Aerial Gaussian Splat)

One of my favourite captures. Flying around Tower Bridge required comprehensive pre-flight authorization—submitting detailed flight plans to the CAA including flight path, timing, and full risk assessment. I registered the flight on DroneDeploy so other pilots could see active operations in the area. On the day of the flight, I contacted the City of London Police to obtain a flight number and confirm the operation. I then flew a complete orbit around Tower Bridge, capturing the iconic structure from every angle before landing.

The result is a complete 3D Gaussian splat representation of one of London's most recognizable landmarks, captured with full regulatory compliance and professional drone operation standards.

Mask (Ultra High-Poly Photogrammetry)

This mask capture demonstrates high-fidelity studio photogrammetry. Using a DSLR camera in a controlled home studio environment with professional lighting, I captured over 500 photographs from different angles as the mask rotated on a turntable. Each photo was color-corrected and distortion-corrected in Lightroom before processing.

The images were processed in Metashape Pro to generate an ultra-high-poly mesh and 8K texture maps. Final cleanup and optimization occurred in Blender, where I applied Draco compression to maintain visual fidelity while dramatically reducing file size for web deployment.

Ilford Mansion (Valentine's Park)

Ilford Mansion in Valentine's Park captured via drone Gaussian splatting. This heritage building required CAA-approved flight operations similar to the Tower Bridge capture, though police notification wasn't required for this location. Multiple orbital passes ensured complete coverage of the mansion's architecture and surrounding grounds.

The resulting Gaussian splat preserves the historic structure in 3D, allowing virtual exploration from perspectives impossible to achieve on foot.
